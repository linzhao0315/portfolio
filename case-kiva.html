Chapter 01 · User Research

Five user types.
Mapped precisely.

Kiva needed SME and high-net-worth partners to adopt its Giving Fund platform — but CSR budgets were shrinking and the conversion funnel was flat. Before ideating anything, we needed a precise picture of who we were designing for and what was blocking each of them.

GPT synthesized 12+ user interviews — collapsing weeks of thematic analysis into hours, surfacing patterns across segments, and refining our user type classifications with a level of consistency that's hard to achieve manually at speed.

GPT synthesized 12+ user interviews — updated and refined user type classifications in hours, not weeks

The insight that shaped everything
"They're not disinterested. They're stuck."
SMEs are willing but blocked — by a gap between their local identity and Kiva's global story. The problem isn't motivation. It's friction. This became the design brief: remove friction, surface impact, make commitment feel obvious.
User types sharp. Strategic brief clear. The question: how do you turn all of this into design direction — fast, with the whole team aligned, without a month-long sprint?
Chapter 02 · AI-Powered Ideation

Two hours. Every designer,
every PM. All ideating with AI.

Instead of a week-long design sprint, the team ran focused AI-powered ideation sessions. Every designer and every PM entered deep, structured conversations with GPT and Claude — not "generate me some ideas," but genuine dialogue aimed at surfacing the features and experiences most likely to hook, engage, convert, and retain users across each segment.

The difference wasn't just speed. It was the quality of the discussion. Ideas that used to take a full day to sketch, circulate, and align on could be visualized and reacted to within the same two-hour session. Prototypes told the story while the meeting was still happening.

What used to take a week of design sprints now happened in a focused 2-hour AI workshop session
Step 01
Deep dialogue, not prompting
Each session started with a user type and an open challenge. GPT and Claude were used as thinking partners, not generators — the AI pushed back, named tensions, and proposed directions the team hadn't considered. Every session ended with a prioritized list of potential features and the user research rationale behind each one. The output was opinionated, not generic.
Step 02
Instant visualization in the room
AI-generated prototypes made ideas tangible during the meeting itself. A layout direction that once needed a day of design work was visible, discussable, and iterable within minutes. The team could point at a real screen, give feedback, and see a revised version — all within the same session. This changed what "alignment" meant: instead of aligning on a description, the team reacted to a real thing.
Workshop output — two layout directions reviewed live, then iterated three passes before the session ended
Step 03
Breadth, then convergence
40+ directions explored across the workshop. AI made breadth of exploration feasible without slowing the team down — because generating a new variant took minutes, not days. The selection criteria was grounded in the research: does this feature solve a documented pain point? Does it hook the right user type at the right moment? AI handled the generation volume. The team handled the judgment.
40+ states explored — the kind of coverage that usually requires a multi-week sprint
The workshop surfaced the features. The research validated the selection. Now: build the designs that will go to user testing.
Chapter 03 · Design for Testing

Features chosen by research,
shaped by AI, ready to test.

From the ideation workshop, a clear set of features emerged — each directly traceable to a user type and a documented friction point. The design decisions weren't intuitions. They were conclusions from 12+ synthesized interviews, structured team ideation, and AI-assisted validation of each direction against the research framework.

01
Impact Multiplier — answers "what does my money actually do?"

The workshop identified this as the top conversion blocker across all five user types. The AI-generated framing that stuck: "$5,000 helps 20 people once — or 50+ people over time through loan recycling." One comparison replaces pages of explanation. Claude helped us test 12 variants of this visualization before we landed here.

One comparison, one action — landed after 12 AI-generated variants tested against user research criteria
02
One framework, five cause areas — configuration, not fragmentation

Rather than five separate product experiences, the team designed one extensible framework that adapts through configuration. AI-assisted prototyping made it possible to rapidly validate whether a single framework could genuinely serve five cause areas — Women, Refugees, Climate, Agriculture, Education — without feeling generic to any of them.

04
Post-conversion — impact made visible

The workshop surfaced a retention insight: the moment after commitment is the moment users are most open to emotional reinforcement. A live impact simulation — real borrower profiles, animated globe, fund allocation in real time — replaces the static "thank you" screen. Large partners use it as marketing collateral; individual donors see the human stories their money reaches. Both behaviours were anticipated in the AI ideation session before a single line of code was written.
