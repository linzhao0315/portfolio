Shoply · Lead UX/UI Designer

From 31%
retention to 82%.

Users downloaded Shoply, poked around for four days, and left. The cashback app's V1 was haemorrhaging users — declining conversion, spiking bounce rates, and a retention curve that flatlined after day four. I owned V2 end to end: audit, redesign, testing, and a new design system.

01 — The Problem

Day 4 was the cliff

The data was unambiguous: users who survived onboarding hit a wall by day four. They couldn't find deals, couldn't figure out redemption, and didn't see the value fast enough. The question wasn't whether to redesign — it was how deep to go.

02 — Research

Two users, opposite needs

Marketing data surfaced two behavioural archetypes — and they wanted fundamentally different things from the same app:

The Deal Hunter — browses online, wants speed
  • Prefers online shopping
  • Goals: save money quickly, earn rewards
  • Wants convenient cashback experience
  • Needs clean, easy-to-scan content
  • Values user-friendly interface for browsing and purchasing
The Coupon Clipper — shops in-store, wants simplicity
  • Prefers offline shopping
  • Goals: save money, find nearby store coupons
  • Needs quick access to deals and coupons
  • Wants easy-to-learn app
  • Simple interface — no tech complexity
03 — Heuristic Audit

Broken at every layer

A heuristic evaluation across onboarding, homepage, and redemption revealed the problems weren't cosmetic — they were structural:

Onboarding
  • Lacks visual appeal — aesthetic and minimalist design failure
  • No feature intro demonstrating product value
  • Excessive pre-sign-up questions without skip button, heightening user anxiety
Homepage
  • No search, filtering, or sorting — users can't find deals
  • Visual hierarchy collapse: icons and text blend together
  • Saved offers disappear with no way to find them again
Redemption
  • Users unsure where to upload receipts — logos not clearly clickable
  • No immediate feedback after receipt upload — no success or error message
04 — Competitive Analysis

What the best cashback apps got right

I benchmarked 8 competitors across 9 dimensions and presented findings company-wide. The gap between Shoply and market leaders (Ibotta, Rakuten) was clear — and specific:

  • Immediate value hook — join bonuses + instruction pages drive first-session engagement
  • Location-aware deals — geo-targeting bridges online and offline shopping
  • Browse by category + sort — navigation that matches how users think about deals
  • Real-time cashback status — visibility of system status keeps users in control
05 — Ideation

Rebuilding the information architecture

Card sorting with stakeholders confirmed what testing showed: users couldn't find deals because the IA didn't match how they think about shopping. I rebuilt the flow with the PM and devs around one core principle: every screen must have a clear next step, and every step must be reversible.

06 — Design

From whiteboard to high-fidelity

Wireframes first, whiteboarding with the team to align on logic and edge cases, then high-fidelity designs targeting the two core failure points:

Onboarding: show value, not process

  • Replaced text walls with visual demos of cashback in action
  • Cut pre-sign-up questions — earn trust first, ask later

Homepage: findability over decoration

  • Added search + category filtering — the #1 user request
  • Rebuilt visual hierarchy so deals, not chrome, dominate the fold
07 — Testing

272 users confirmed the direction

338 users recruited, 272 completed usability tests across three core tasks. Alongside that, 10+ think-aloud sessions with a researcher gave us the qualitative "why" behind the numbers:

78%
found it "easy to use, simple, clean, modern"
81%
found it "easy to learn"
72%
wanted to use it frequently

I distilled the top 10 pain points into prioritised recommendations, weighed against engineering feasibility and timeline. V2 shipped the critical fixes; the remaining insights seeded V3 strategy.

09 — Outcomes

The retention curve bent

V2 didn't just fix individual screens — it fixed the system. Every heuristic failure from the audit got a targeted solution:

31→82%
user retention improvement
272
usability test completions
2.0
new design system shipped
  • Visibility of System Status: Instant confirmation after adding deals and uploading receipts
  • Consistency & Standards: New UI library clarifying information hierarchy
  • Error Prevention: Error messages with instructions for fixing issues
  • Aesthetic & Minimalist Design: Sorting and filtering for a concise, logical homepage

The real lesson: V1 failed not because individual screens were bad, but because no one owned the system. Icons, hierarchy, feedback loops, redemption flows — they were designed in isolation. V2 worked because I treated the entire app as one connected experience, not a collection of screens. A design system isn't a style guide — it's the connective tissue between every interaction.
